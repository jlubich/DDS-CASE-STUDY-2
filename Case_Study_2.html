Header 1

DDSAnalytics is an analytic company that specializes in talent management solutions for Fortune 1000 companies. Having access to valuable demographic information obtained from employees personnel files and workplace environmental data (Employee Surveys), DDSAnalytics executive management is interested leveraging this wealth of data, to provide additional services to their client base and assist them in improving their talent management strategies.
DDSAnalytics management understands the key role an excellent talent management system play in the development of any company. Untold amounts of money and resources are spent hiring, training, and developing the most valuable asset a company has; Their Employees. The path is fraught with missteps, from hiring the wrong fit personnel, not identifying and nurturing young talent, loss of productivity due to inexperienced personnel and lower profits due to excessive expenses associated with recruiting new employees.
Purpose: Through the use of Data Science, DDSAnalytics identified an opportunity to gain a competitive edge by helping their clients better manage their talent. Before management green lights the development of the data science department, they have tasked the data science(DS) team to conduct an analysis of existing employee data. The executive leadership has identified predicting employee turnover as its first application for the data science initiative. The DS team was presented an existing data-set, to conduct an exploratory data analysis (EDA), to determine factor that leads to employee attrition. The major objectives are as follows:

  • Identify and discuss the top factors that contribute to employee attrition.
  • Determine if any job role specific trends that may exist.
  • Identify any interesting trends and/or observations
  • Determine Next-Steps in EDA process.

Dataset

  • Client Dataset: The DS team was presented with an anonymous clients employee data-set. Certain identifiable information was removed from the data-set (employee names, company name, etc.). The data-set contains 35 variables with 1470 observations. The variables captured both employee data (age, sex, tenure, position, wages, performance reviews, education,) as well as workplace environmental data (Job involvement, job satisfaction, work/life balance, travel%,), which provides a more complete picture of the workers and their environment.

Baseline Attrition Rate: Little Write up here on how we compute it.

EmployeeCount.Total = sum(TalentData$EmployeeCount)
AttritionCount.Total = sum(TalentData$AttritionCount)

AttritionRate.Percent = round(AttritionCount.Total / EmployeeCount.Total, 2) * 100 
## Here's the magic function which computes the Attrition Rates, and Counts
fun.AttritionRate <- function(input.table, group.by.column) {
  input.table %>%
    dplyr::group_by(CategoryVar = input.table[,group.by.column]) %>%
    dplyr::summarise(
      AttritionRate = sum(AttritionCount)/sum(EmployeeCount), 
      AttritionCount = sum(AttritionCount),
      EmployeeCount = sum(EmployeeCount)) %>%
    dplyr::arrange(CategoryVar)
}

## Choose the columns which make sense
ColumnsToAnalyze <- c(
  which( colnames(TalentData)=="NumberOfCompaniesWorked" ),
  which( colnames(TalentData)=="BusinessTravel" ),
  which( colnames(TalentData)=="Department" ),
  which( colnames(TalentData)=="EducationField" ),
  which( colnames(TalentData)=="Gender" ),
  which( colnames(TalentData)=="MaritalStatus" ),
  which( colnames(TalentData)=="JobRole" ),
  which( colnames(TalentData)=="OverTime" ),
  grep("*Label", names(TalentData)), 
  grep("*Bin", names(TalentData))
)


#TalentData <- as_tibble(TalentData)

## Initilize a list to hold all the attrition rate details
Attrition.List = list()

## Loop through all of the columns in TalentData and get the attrition rate details
for(i in ColumnsToAnalyze){
  #Get the name of the current column 
  SummaryColumn <- names(TalentData)[i]
  #Get the attrition rate details into a DF
  SummaryResults <- fun.AttritionRate(TalentData, i)
  #Concatenate the name of the current column to the details
  SummaryResults$ColumnName <- SummaryColumn
  #Store the details into a list with all other column details
  Attrition.List[[i]] <- SummaryResults
}  

## Create a single data frame out of all of the detail results
attrition_data = do.call(rbind, Attrition.List)
